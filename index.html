01 · About

The person behind the dashboard

I have spent the last decade at the intersection of people and product, helping communities feel like home while quietly wiring up the systems that keep them healthy. From open source projects to large-scale events, my work has always started with a simple question: how can this space feel safer, clearer, and more engaging for the next person who walks in?

I am most at ease translating between humans and systems, turning long forum threads into a roadmap, and transforming contributor discomfort into specific, actionable fixes. Whether I am running a secret-shopper style audit of an OSS project, designing contributor guidelines, or shipping a small tool, the goal is the same: more signal, less friction, better stories.

These days, I split my time between community management, community health consulting through Commit Confidential, and building small tools and add-ons that make it easier to contribute, collaborate, and celebrate wins together.
